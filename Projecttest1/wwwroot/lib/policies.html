Our Policies

Options Policy

The Autism Society promotes active and informed involvement of family members and the individual with autism in the planning of individualized, appropriatte services and support. The board of the Autism Society believes that each person with autism is a unique individual. Each family and individual with autism should have the right to learn about and then select the options that they feel are the most appropriate for the individual with autism. to the maximum extent possible, we believe that both the parents and the individual with autism should make decisions.
Services should enhance and strengthen natural family and community supports for the individual with autism and the family whenever possible. The service option designed for and individual with autism should result improved quality of life. Abusive treatment of any kind is not an option.

Our Term

The terms "autism" and "pervasive development disorder" are sometimes used interchangeably on this site. Although some people distinguish between these two diagnoses, the same educational strategies and interventions will help children with either diagnosis.

Our Privacy

AcadianaAutism.or does not collect personal information from users of this site. It does not send unsolicited e-mail or spam through its email services. ALL recipients of email sent through the bulk email service we use, Constant Contact, have given their consent to receive email form Autism Society Acadiana. If you do not wish to remain on our email list, please follow the instructions to unsubscribe, or contact our office to have your email address removed from the list.
